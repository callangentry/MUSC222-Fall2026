    Course Description
    🔗
    An introduction to the hardware, software, and techniques involved with composing, editing and producing digital music. The primary goal is to gain a working facility and knowledge of fundamental technologies found within a modern music production environment such as digital audio workstation (DAW) software, file management, mixing techniques, sound editing, sequencing, sampling, sound design, and synthesis. Foremost is the creative application of these skills that result in original pieces of music, sound design environments, and emulated modular synthesizer design.

    Section Course Work

    Subsection Listening Responses

    These are designed to help you develop a critical ear for the familiar music that surrounds you and (perhaps) unfamiliar music you encounter in class.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection In-Class Activities

    These are designed to apply what you are learning at a moment’s notice. They cannot be done outside class time.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Assignments

    Each one of these assignments are designed as part of the workflow for each project within each unit. They focus on technique, file management, and working with musical ideas. They must be handed in on time.
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection Projects

    Project #1: Sound Collage A short 1–2 minute piece that comes from sounds you’ve recorded, edited, and manipulated from the world around (outside in nature, in your home, at work, in the city, etc.). You must come up with an original title.
    🔗
    Project #2: POP!! A short 2–3 minute piece of music that uses standard instrumental layers, textures, and forms used in pop/rock music composed through software instruments using MIDI. You will be able to improve your score through following instructor feedback.
    🔗
    Project #3: Patchtastic For this project you will demonstrate your knowledge of synthesis, signal chain, sequencing, and generative operations in a virtual modular environment found in VCV Rack. You will be scored on patch organization and tidiness as well as the creative outcomes (some of which you will record). You will be able to improve your score through following instructor feedback.
    🔗
    Final Project: Composition This project is the culmination of everything you did throughout the semester. The piece should last at minimum 3 minutes. It will be played during class in front of your peers as part of the final exam.
    🔗

    1. Emergency situation that keeps you from handing it in, or

    Subsection Course Expectations

    You must always attend class and ON TIME since that is when most of the work and tutorials are happening. You will not succeed in this class if you do not attend. All assignments can be improved but only if they are handed in on time so you can receive proper feedback. No phones in class time.

    Subsection Academic Honesty and Generative AI

    Integrity is essential to academic life. Consequently, students who enroll at Framingham State University agree to maintain high standards of academic honesty and scholarly practice. They shall be responsible for familiarizing themselves with the published policies and procedures regarding academic honesty. See Academic Honesty Policy here.
    🔗
    Generative AI only creates text and music from the pre-existing work of other humans. Since this course centers around your personal development of creative and listening skills, using chatbots will serve no purpose. Simply put, a bot cannot do the work for you. If I suspect any Generative AI mischief (especially with the Listening Responses), I will give you an opportunity to rewrite; otherwise, you will receive a zero and may be subject to academic discipline.
